# Security Policy

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you discover a security vulnerability in this website or its supporting infrastructure, please report it responsibly by emailing:

**dehaasjulian@gmail.com**

Use the subject line: `[SECURITY] BestCheesecakeInTheWorld – <brief description>`

Please **do not** open a public GitHub issue for security vulnerabilities.

---

## What to Include in Your Report

To help us understand and resolve the issue quickly, please include as much of the following as possible:

- **Description** — a clear explanation of the vulnerability and its potential impact
- **Affected URL or component** — e.g. `bestcheesecakeintheworld.com`, `/api/cheesecakes.json`, a specific page or feature
- **Steps to reproduce** — a minimal, step-by-step sequence to trigger the issue
- **Proof of concept** — screenshots, a video recording, or example requests/responses (redact any sensitive data)
- **Suggested severity** — your assessment of Critical / High / Medium / Low based on impact and exploitability
- **Your name or handle** — if you would like to be credited in any public acknowledgement (optional)

---

## What Happens Next

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Within **48 hours** | We confirm receipt of your report and begin triage |
| Within **7 days** | We share our initial assessment and an estimated resolution timeline |
| Within **30 days** | We aim to have a fix deployed (complex issues may take longer — we will keep you informed) |
| After fix | We will notify you so you can verify the resolution, and credit you if desired |

We will keep you updated throughout the process. If you do not hear back within 48 hours, please follow up at the same email address.

---

## Scope

The following are **in scope** for responsible disclosure:

- The website hosted at `bestcheesecakeintheworld.com`
- The public JSON API at `/api/cheesecakes.json`
- Any GitHub Pages configuration or content injection vectors

The following are **out of scope**:

- Vulnerabilities in GitHub itself (report those to [GitHub Security](https://github.com/security))
- Attacks requiring physical access to our devices
- Social engineering or phishing attacks targeting the site owners
- Denial-of-service attacks

---

## Our Commitments

We commit to:

- Acknowledging your report promptly
- Keeping you informed of our progress
- Not pursuing legal action against researchers who follow this policy in good faith
- Crediting responsible disclosers publicly if they wish

We ask that you:

- Give us a reasonable time to fix the issue before any public disclosure
- Avoid accessing, modifying, or deleting data that does not belong to you
- Limit your testing to demonstrating the existence of the vulnerability

---

## Supported Versions

This is a continuously deployed static website. Only the current live version at `bestcheesecakeintheworld.com` is actively maintained.
